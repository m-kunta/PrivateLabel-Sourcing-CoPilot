# How to Use: PL Sourcing Co-Pilot

Welcome to the **Private Label Sourcing Co-Pilot**. This document provides a comprehensive guide on how to set up the environment, run the dashboard, interpret the AI's analysis, and apply these insights directly in your day-to-day supplier negotiations and category planning.

---

## 1. Project Setup

Follow these steps to get the Co-Pilot running on your local machine.

### Prerequisites
*   **Python 3.9+** installed on your system.
*   **API Keys:** At minimum, you need one LLM API key (e.g., Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, or Groq). 
*   **Pinecone Account (Optional but Recommended):** For the full RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) experience, sign up for a free tier at [pinecone.io](https://pinecone.io).

### Installation
Open your terminal and clone or navigate to the project directory:
```bash
cd pl_sourcing_copilot

# Create and activate a virtual environment
python3 -m venv .venv
source .venv/bin/activate  # On Windows use: .venv\Scripts\activate

# Install the required dependencies
pip install -r requirements.txt
```

### Configuration
You must provide the application with your API keys using an environment file.
1. Copy the example environment file:
   ```bash
   cp .env.example .env
   ```
2. Open `.env` in a text editor and fill in your keys:
   ```env
   PINECONE_API_KEY=your_pinecone_key_here
   LLM_PROVIDER=Anthropic
   ANTHROPIC_API_KEY=your_anthropic_key_here
   ```

### Data Initialization
Before using the app, you need to generate the synthetic baseline data and load it into the system.

1. **Generate the Data:** Run the generator script in your terminal:
   ```bash
   python data_gen.py
   ```
   *This creates `data/vendor_lead_times.csv` with 50 rows of synthetic private-label sourcing data.*

2. **Launch the Dashboard:**
   ```bash
   streamlit run app.py
   ```
   *Your browser will automatically open to `http://localhost:8501`.*

3. **Ingest to Pinecone:**
   *   Navigate to the **🗄️ Data Hub** tab in the browser.
   *   Click **Initialize Pinecone Index** (this takes about 60 seconds).
   *   Once initialized, click **Ingest Vendor Lead Times**.
   *   Finally, click **Ingest Live News Disruptions** to pull in the latest supply chain events.

## 2. Using the Dashboard

The Co-Pilot is divided into three main tabs:

### 📊 Scenario Analyzer (Your Daily Workspace)
This is where you ask the AI "what-if" questions.
*   **Input Box:** Type a natural language disruption scenario (e.g., *"A port strike is declared at Savannah. Which of our private label components are critically delayed?"*).
*   **Quick Toggles:** Click one of the pre-configured scenario buttons on the right (like the "Hormuz Event Toggle") to instantly populate the input box with a complex geopolitical scenario.
*   **Analyze Button:** Click "Analyze Scenario" to trigger the vector search and LLM reasoning.

### 📈 Risk Dashboard
A visual reporting tab for high-level category management.
*   **Lead Time Volatility:** Scatter plot showing historical variance across different product categories.
*   **Chokepoint Exposure:** A bar chart showing how many of your components rely on vulnerable routes like the Panama Canal or the Strait of Hormuz.

### 🗄️ Data Hub
Your administration panel. Use this tab to refresh your Pinecone index with the latest lead times and pull in breaking RSS news feeds.

## 3. Reading the Output

After running a scenario in the Scenario Analyzer, the Co-Pilot generates a comprehensive report divided into three key sections:

### A. Strategic Analyst Briefing
This is a C-suite ready summary generated by the LLM. 
*   **Executive Summary:** A high-level overview of what the disruption is and how it impacts your portfolio.
*   **Key Findings:** Bullet points detailing the specific routes and categories affected.
*   **Recommended Actions:** Immediate steps the buying team should take to mitigate risk.
*   **Risk Horizon:** An estimate of how long the disruption will last (e.g., "4–8 weeks").

### B. Risk Impact Table
A sortable, color-coded grid mapping the exact impact on your raw materials.
*   🟢 **Green:** Adjusted lead time is less than a 15% increase. Normal operations.
*   🟡 **Yellow:** Adjusted lead time is between 15% and 35% higher. Monitor closely and consider safety stock.
*   🔴 **Red:** Adjusted lead time is > 35% higher. Immediate mitigation required.
*   *Note: Use the **Download CSV** button to export this table and share it with your planning team.*

### C. Ripple Effects
Supply chains are interconnected. This section traces second-order impacts. For example, it won't just tell you that cotton is delayed at Savannah; it will point out that the spandex fiber and packaging materials on that same route are also delayed, risking your entire apparel line.

## 4. How to Use the Output in Actual Supplier Meetings

The PL Sourcing Co-Pilot isn't just an analytical tool; it's a strategic lever for supplier negotiations and category planning. Here is how to use it in the real world:

### Scenario Planning & Quarterly Reviews
*   **Pre-Meeting Prep:** Before a Quarterly Business Review (QBR) with a major vendor, run scenarios against their specific shipping routes. 
*   **The Conversation:** Bring the exported Risk Table to the meeting. *"Our AI analysis shows that your routing through Savannah puts 60% of our Spring apparel line at a 🔴 Red risk level if the ILA strikes next month. What is your contingency routing plan?"*

### Proactive Renegotiation & Cost Avoidance
*   **Leverage:** If the Co-Pilot's Ripple Effects show that a vendor's supply chain is highly vulnerable to a brewing geopolitical event (e.g., Red Sea detours), use this data to negotiate shared risk.
*   **The Conversation:** *"We've modeled the Cape of Good Hope detours. Because this adds 14 days to your lead time, we require you to hold an additional 2 weeks of safety stock domestically at no carrying cost to us, or we will need to split the emergency air freight costs."*

### Diversification Strategies
*   **Sourcing Audits:** Use the Risk Dashboard to identify over-reliance on a single chokepoint. 
*   **The Conversation (Internal):** Bring the Dashboard charts to your Category VP. *"We are over-exposed to the Panama Canal for our furniture components. We need to mandate that our top 3 vendors dual-source raw materials from Mexico or Eastern Europe to balance this profile."*

### Crisis Response
*   **Immediate Action:** When news breaks (e.g., a canal blockage), click the "Ingest Live News Disruptions" button, type the headline into the Scenario Analyzer, and export the CSV.
*   **The Action:** Hand the CSV directly to your inventory planners within 5 minutes of the news breaking so they can immediately issue purchase orders for alternative goods before your competitors react.
